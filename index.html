Executive recommendation — EBITDA stress & margin risk

The simulation shows that under current market conditions the probability of EBITDA falling below USD 7.3B — the 18th percentile threshold representing meaningful downside stress — exceeds 18% in any given year. At default parameters the VaR 95% floor is approximately USD 5.8B. Board and ExCo planning should use the VaR 95% figure as the budget floor, not expected EBITDA. The COGS ratio is the single most powerful lever: at a base revenue of USD 57B, each 1pp COGS reduction releases approximately USD 570M of EBITDA, directly lifting the VaR floor. Where COGS reduction is constrained by commodity prices, a pricing or revenue-mix lever should be pursued as an alternative.

01
Set VaR 95% as budget floor
Owner: CFO. Replace expected-value planning with VaR 95% as the minimum EBITDA assumption for capex and dividend decisions.
02
Activate FX hedge programme
Owner: Group Treasurer. Hedge minimum 60% of USD/CNY and USD/EUR exposure 12 months forward. See Hedge Analyser for quantification.
03
COGS reduction programme
Owner: COO/CFO. Each 1pp COGS improvement shifts VaR floor by ~USD 570M (base revenue USD 57B × 1%). Target 81% COGS ratio by Q4 2026 through supply chain renegotiation. Where commodity-driven, pursue pricing or revenue-mix lever as alternative.
04
Board ask — Q3 2026 planning cycle
Board is requested to direct the CFO to anchor all capital allocation, dividend, and covenant decisions to the VaR 95% EBITDA figure with effect from the Q3 2026 planning cycle. Expected-value planning understates downside risk by design.

Executive recommendation — FX hedging strategy

A 60% forward hedge at a rate above spot simultaneously reduces tail risk and adds expected value. CVaR improvement is proportionally larger than VaR improvement — the hedge compresses severity in the worst-case scenarios, protecting covenant compliance precisely when it matters most. Current unhedged FX exposure of USD 420M breaches the F-01 tolerance limit of USD 350M, requiring a Board treasury policy exception. Basis risk note: if actual FX-exposed revenue falls more than 10% below the hedged volume, the organisation holds an unintended short position at the forward rate — Treasury must flag any material revenue shortfall within 30 days of detection. Use a minimum of two counterparty banks to avoid concentration in the hedge book itself.

01
Execute 60% hedge immediately
Owner: Group Treasurer. Authorise 12-month forward contracts on USD/CNY and USD/EUR exposure. Reduces VaR by 30–45%.
02
Set hard review trigger
Owner: CFO. If forward rate moves >5% adverse, convene Treasury Committee within 3 business days. No auto-roll at unfavourable rates.
03
Monthly basis risk monitoring
Owner: Group Treasurer. Track actual revenue vs hedged volume. Report to CFO within 30 days if deviation exceeds 10% — creates an unintended short position at the forward rate. Use minimum two counterparty banks.
04
Board ask — treasury policy exception
Board is requested to grant a treasury policy exception authorising forward contract execution up to USD 300M notional, noting that current unhedged exposure of USD 420M already breaches the USD 350M F-01 tolerance limit.

Executive recommendation — supply chain resilience

O-01 is at red breach — single-source concentration at 68% against a 50% red threshold. Board action is required immediately. The simulation confirms the compound scenario (supplier failure + demand shock) occurs in approximately 1 in 6 years at default parameters (P(failure) 58% × P(demand shock) 30% = 17.4% p.a.), producing losses exceeding USD 500M in 18% of simulations. The dual-source programme delivers the highest VaR reduction per dollar spent (central estimate: USD 95M VaR saving at USD 15M/yr — a 6× return). The strategic inventory buffer is fastest to implement. Insurance is the cheapest add-on once the first two are in place.
