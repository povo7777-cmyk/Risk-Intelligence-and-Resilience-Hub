Risk Committee Summary
Run 20260525 · 2026-05-25 08:44 UTC
Risk Posture
This period shows material control stress: 10 KRI breaches and 17 amber findings across four domains, all of which are in breach. Escalation to the board is required. The aggregate picture is one of compound exposure where operational fragility, financial covenant pressure, and compliance gaps create cascading failure pathways. The combination of unhedged currency exposure concentrated in geopolitically sensitive jurisdictions, inventory depletion to breach threshold at single-source suppliers showing financial distress, degraded cyber response capability, and deteriorating credit quality ahead of covenant testing creates a nexus of timing risk. Management response is in place across all material findings.
Key Risk Drivers
The most consequential combination this period is the intersection of supply chain brittleness, covenant proximity, and cyber control degradation.
Inventory cover has breached threshold at 3.9 weeks against an 18-week TSMC lead time, while 62.6% single-source concentration includes a supplier in financial distress.
Simultaneously, bad debt provision stands at 1.44% against a 0.8% threshold, with USD 60M overdue beyond 90 days — both feeding directly into EBITDA at a point where Net Debt/EBITDA sits at 2.8 with only 0.2 headroom to the 3.0 covenant threshold tested on 2026-06-30.
Cyber response capability is impaired: 71% patch compliance, 11-day mean time to detect, and 7 critical IT-Operations roles unfilled for over 60 days reduce the organisation's ability to recover trading platform systems within the required 6-hour window.
This matters because a supply disruption that cannot be detected or managed due to unavailable systems, or a credit loss that crystallises ahead of covenant testing, or a cyber incident that extends beyond recovery tolerance due to understaffing — each individually stresses the organisation, but in combination they compress decision time and eliminate recovery options.
Cross-Domain Connections
Unhedged FX exposure of USD 4,940M is concentrated in USD/CNY (USD 1,800M) and USD/KRW (USD 300M), both currencies in jurisdictions where 5 geopolitical signals have been detected, 4 at high severity.
Current unrealised losses stand at USD 47M, already eroding the USD 300M liquidity headroom above covenant minimums.
If currency volatility increases in line with geopolitical stress, mark-to-market losses crystallise against the unhedged portion and compress liquidity headroom further at the same moment covenant testing occurs.
The supply chain exposure to a single-source supplier in financial distress (health score 61, carrying the highest rejection rate at 1.2%) intersects with cyber risk: if demand visibility or order management systems are unavailable during a supply disruption — given 11-day detection time and 18-day remediation time — the organisation cannot reorder or redirect inventory for 29 days, a period longer than current inventory cover.
The 22.1% revenue concentration in a single customer within Enterprise Tier1, combined with deteriorating credit quality in Tier2 and SMB segments, means that loss of the top customer or unexpected bad debt write-off reduces EBITDA by 3-4% and triggers covenant breach at a point where only 0.2 ratio points of headroom exist.
